What is MLA referencing?

Modern Language Association or MLA is an organization that has standardized how scholars will cite sources in their work. The MLA 8th edition citation style is often used in subject areas like literature, languages, art, life sciences, music. theatre, social science, history, economics, mathematics and statistics etc. Many schools, colleges and universities in Australia recommend that students use this citation format.

Why is there a need to Reference?

As a scholar cites the resources in their work, it becomes easier for the readers to understand what kind of sources were used in the project. The readers might look through those resources to understand the concept of your write-up in a better way.

Citation is a good way to acknowledge other authors that you have borrowed their content. In other words, you are giving them credit for their work.

Citation and Monash referencing also save you from plagiarism. Don't be surprised! If you are using someone else's work in your paper without any reference then this is considered duplicity or simply stated - PLAGIARISM! So why get your paper rejected? How to cite a BLOG in latest MLA referencing style?

Author's Last Name, Author's First Name. "Title of Post." Blog Name, Publisher (mentioned only when the name of Publisher is different than the name of the blog), Date of Publishing the blog post, Link to post (omit // or //).

Smith, John. "How to Write a Thesis in 10 Steps." GoAssignmentHelp, 25 Dec. 2019, www.goassignmenthelp.com/blog/how-to-write-a-thesis-in-10-steps/.

How to Cite a BOOK CHAPTER in MLA referencing style

Author's Surname, Given Names. "Title of Chapter or Part." Title: Subtitles of Book. Edition (if not the first), edited by Editor Name, Publisher, Year, pp. Pages.

Smith, John. "The Essence of Routines." The Power of Habits, edited by Jack Williams, MacMillan Press, 1998, pp. 23-89.

How to cite a DISSERTATION in MLA referencing style

Author Surname, Author First Name. 'Title'. Level. Institution Name, Year Published. Print (if the Journal is in printed form).

Smith, John, and Williams, Jack. 'Do Young People Discuss Their Mental Health Issues Freely'. RMIT, 1999. Print.

How to cite an E-BOOK in MLA referencing style

Author's last name, Author's First name. "Title of the chapter or section." Title of the e-book, translated by or edited by First name Last name, vol. number, Publisher, Year of publication, page number(s). Title of the web site or database, URL.

Smith, John. Quantum Physics: A New Approach, 2015. Google Books, books.google.com/books?id=x2xYaYeZwmeIV&ghj=OOL1&ef=&pg=PP1#v=onepage&q=physics=false.

Citing Ebook from an EReader

Author's last name, Author's First name. "Title of the chapter or section." Title of the e-book, translated by or edited by First name Last name, Name of e-reader device, vol. number, Publisher, Year of publication, page number(s).

Smith, John. "A Christmas Carol." The New Year Book, Kindle ed., Scribner, 2011, pp 35-67.

How to cite an E-JOURNAL in MLA referencing style

Author's Last name, Author's First name. "Title of the article." Title of the journal, First name Last name of any other contributors (if applicable), Version (if applicable), Numbers (such as a volume and issue number), Publication date, Page numbers. Title of the database, URL or DOI.

Simon, Roger, et al. "City Public Library: Challenges of New Age." Information and Technology Libraries, vol. 25, no. 3, Mar. 2001, pp. 6-18. QUEST,

ezproxy.cpl.org/login?url=http://search.challaengesofmewage.com.i.ezproxy.cpl.org/docview/334561234?accountid=128546.

How to cite a GOVERNMENT PUBLICATION in MLA referencing style

Last, First [or] Government Department. Title of Document. Location: Publisher, Year published. Print.

Melbourne Department of Childcare and Nursing. Directory of Resources. Melbourne: XYW, 1950. Print.

How to cite a JOURNAL in MLA referencing style

Last Name, First Name., and First Name. Last Name. "Article Title." Journal Title Series Volume. Issue (Year Published): Page(s). Print.

Smith, John. "Dissertation Format and Research Methodology." Melbourne Journal of Academic Writing 44 (2009): 673-800. Print.

How to cite a MAGAZINE in MLA referencing style

Last Name, First Name. "Article Title." Magazine Name Publication Date: Page Numbers. Medium.

Smith, John. "How Students Should Spend Leisure Time." Melbourne Student Book Nov.2019: 19-21. Print.

How to cite a NEWSPAPER in MLA referencing style

Last Name, First Name. "Article Title." Newspaper Name Publication Date: Page Numbers. Medium.

Smith, John. "How ATAR Scores Affect Your Career." Herald Sun 12 June 2018: 7-9. Print.

How to cite a WEBSITE in MLA referencing style

Last Name, First Name. "Page Title." Website Title. Sponsoring Institution/Publisher. Publication Date: Page Numbers. Medium.

Smith, John. "How ATAR Scores Affect Your Career" HeraldSun.org. Herald Sun Networks, 12 June. 2018. Web.

MLA referencing in-text Citation with an Example

Till now we have seen examples of how you can include MLA references in the bibliography. But, do you know that you can also cite a source within the text? Such a citation format is brief and is called an in-text citation.

In-text citations are useful when you are summarizing a source, paraphrasing the source, or including a quote from a source.

What to do

Citation Example

Give the author's last name.

(Smith 22" 45)

Give both author's last names.

(Smith and Arnold 22" 45)

3+ authors

Name the first author followed by "et al."

(Smith et al. 22-45)

Corporate author

If a source was created by an organization other than the publisher, use the organization name as the author.

(Melbourne Welfare Program 22)

No author

If the author is the same as the publisher, or if no author is credited, use the source title instead. Format the title the same as in the full Works Cited reference, and shorten if it is more than four words.

("Australia Economy and Politics")

Multiple sources by the same author

Include the title (or a shortened version) after the author's name in each source citation.

(Smith, Samy, 22)

(Smith, Samy, 45)

What is an In-Text Citation or Parenthetical Citation?

You might have seen two kinds of in-text MLA citation examples:

Example 1: Smith conducted a study on how ATAR scores are affecting students' career choices (123).

Example 2: A study was conducted to analyse what factors contributed to a good ATAR score for students of different educational levels. (Smith, 123).

As you can see In-Text MLA Citation can be done in two ways:

  • Narrative Citation

When you write the name of the author in the sentence it is called Narrative Citation. In such citations, the page number where the information is found at the source is mentioned in parentheses after the sentence.

  • Parenthetical Citation

When you do not mention the Author's name in the sentence in which you are referring to an external source and provide the Author's name along with the page name in parentheses after the sentence, it is known as parenthetical citation.

Can you guess from the above MLA referencing style example, which in-text MLA citation is narrative and which is parenthetical?
