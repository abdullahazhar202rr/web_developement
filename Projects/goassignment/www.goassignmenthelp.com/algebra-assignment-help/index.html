Algebra is a branch of math in which variables and other general symbols are used to represent a number and quantities in formula and equations. Most often while solving Algebraic problems, students get stuck due to their learning and skill gaps in the subject.

An overview of Algebra:

Algebra is just about finding the unknown variable that follows approved rules or it is about putting real-life problems into specific equations and then solving them with the help of algebraic formulas and theorems. Some of the streams of Algebra are as follows:

  • Elementary Algebra: It involves the basic concepts of Algebra that are normally taught at the high school level. It is just an expanded form of arithmetic. The only difference here is that Arithmetic deals with fixed numbers and at an elementary level, algebra deals with generic numbers.
  • Linear Algebra: It involves the study of linear sets of equations and their transformation properties. Learning this branch helps the analysis of rotations in space, solution of coupled differential equations, determining the circle passing through the three given points, least-square fitting, and many complex problems in math, engineering, and applied physics.
  • Abstract Algebra: It is modern Algebra that deals with various algebraic arrangements like vectors, set theories, rings, groups, and many more.

Besides, Algebra has many other applications that have great associations in different fields of studies.

A synopsis of Algebraic equations:

  • Linear equations: The equations that deal with two variables and give a straight line when plotted on a graph are called linear equations.
  • Polynomials equations: These equations deal with two or more variables in higher degrees.

Besides the above two, there are many other types of equations found in higher algebra that have their specific importance like a functional equation, integral equation, differential equation, and many more.

Importance of Algebra:

Algebra is an important and interesting branch for students because as the level of math goes higher, students find that every concept is related to algebra in one way or the other. Even in the other streams of science, especially in Physics and Applied Physics like kinematics, mechanics, astronomy, wave theories, etc. application of algebra is imminent.

Even in different real-life issues, Algebra is important. Understanding varied functions, graphing ordered pairs, solving single and double step equations, and conducting a vertical line test are some of the Algebraic skills used in many real-life activities. So, students will always find a connection between algebraic functions and real-life activities.
