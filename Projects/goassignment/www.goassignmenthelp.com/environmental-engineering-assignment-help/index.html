What is Environmental Engineering all about?

Environmental Engineering is the study of the quality of air, water, and soil. Its primary concern is to provide healthy living conditions. A career in environmental engineering offers opportunities such as conducting original research, writing development plans, and protecting human health.

The following are some of the major branches of Environmental Engineering in which we offer guidance to interested students:

  • Industrial Pollution Control 
  • Solid Waste Management 
  • Air Pollution Control and Noise Abatement 
  • Water Treatment and Wastewater Engineering
  • Environmental Remediation 
  • Applied Microbiology and Biotechnology

We have helped students with Environmental Engineering projects in which they have to design ways to treat water and control pollution. They involve planning methods for disposing of hazardous waste, such as nuclear waste; helping manufacturers reduce air pollution, and controlling emissions from cars and other sources.

Some of the Environmental Engineering assignments centered around the restoration of contaminated land. Their purpose is to ensure that waste does not pollute water supplies or harm plants, animals, or people living near landfills.
