Can Someone Do My Statistics Homework?

We study lots of things in statistics, like samples and populations. We try to answer our research questions using inferential statistical methods (like hypothesis testing). We also use descriptive stats (like means, medians, standard deviations) to give us clues about the population.

But there are two main focuses when it comes to studying Statistics:

  • Population studies, and
  • Sampling studies.

In population studies, we try to describe the population as a whole--that is, all of its elements and how they work together. Population data can be hard to collect because it's often expensive or time-consuming to do so (especially in cases where there are very few people or when the people are scattered across a large area). However, because this data provides the best information about the group as a whole, it's necessary for certain types of research.

Population data is usually collected by an agency like the census bureau or another type of government organization--for example, when they study unemployment rates in states or average incomes per race.

Sampling studies, on the other hand, are done when it's too time-consuming or expensive to gather data about the entire population. Instead of asking every student what their GPA is, you could ask a subset of students--a random sample--instead. This would be faster to do and probably provide similar information to what you'd get from the entire population. But because you didn't ask everyone, it's possible that the data is not representative of the whole population and could be slightly different from what you'd see. 

In Statistics, we try to help make sure this doesn't happen, by controlling for certain factors when we design a study or choose a random sample of elements from a population.

There are lots of sub-fields in statistics, like:

  • categorical data analysis, 
  • statistical computing, and 
  • Bayesian inference.

Why do I find it difficult to write my Statistics homework?

  • Not knowing how to solve a problem

This is one of the most common problems students face while writing Statistics homework. Students usually know what statistics they should use but don't know how to go about doing it. If this is your case, then try explaining the steps you would follow to arrive at the solution rather than just telling what statistic you would use.

For example, if one of the questions asks you to "find the mean age of men who are currently enrolled in medical school", then rather than saying you would take a simple random sample of men who are currently enrolled in medical school, list all of the steps you would follow to find their mean age.

  • Not knowing which formulas to use

Another common problem faced by students is not knowing which formulas to use and where to find them. If you feel like this might be the case with your assignment, then look for similarities between questions in your homework and previous Statistics problems that you have studied. Sometimes we forget we know a particular formula/procedure already when we haven't used it in a while.

  • Not knowing how to solve a problem using the formula

This is another very common issue students face when writing Statistics homework. Most of the time, students know what statistic they need and how to find it but struggle with actually solving their questions using that statistic. This usually happens because we can remember the formula and how to find it but we forget that we need to plug certain values into our formula before using it. So, simply remembering the right formula is not enough for writing good Statistics homework.

  • Not knowing how to solve a problem with technology

While this might seem like a trivial point, it often happens that students don't know how to use technology for their Statistics homework. Many programs carry out statistical functions, but students often ignore them because they don't know how to use them. For example, Excel has a function "=NORMSINV(rand())" which returns the inverse of the standard normal distribution. If you are not sure what this means, then Google it to find out.

  • Not knowing where to look for help

This is one of the biggest problems students face when writing Statistics homework - they are not sure where to find answers!
