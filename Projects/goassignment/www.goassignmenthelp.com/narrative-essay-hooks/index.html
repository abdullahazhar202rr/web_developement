Narrative Essay Hooks

Often, we have all been in a situation, commonly known as a writer’s block. In such a situation, we find our writer-self lost, somewhere deep down in that abyss of lack of motivation. We seem to have a great story, however, we do not know where to begin, what to write, or even how to go about it. Worry not, we offer you the fool-proof narrative essay hook that will help you swim your way out of this situation.

To learn more on Narrative Essay and types of narrative essay examples, click here ⟹ Narrative Essays

What is the Narrative?

At the outset, it is imperative to first know what a narrative is. Fortunately for us, it doesn’t necessarily have to have some moral; it’s one of those simple writings. You don’t have to take sides, argue, or prove something. You just have to narrate. There’s just one catch here. Whatever you narrate ought to be interesting and engaging so that the reader is succumbed to read the complete Narrative.

What is a Narrative Essay Hook?

The first impression is often the last. Whether or not your reader will read your narrative, largely depends upon how you start it. Your narrative will be read-only if the beginning is compelling enough. As promised, we’re here to help you out. Here are some of the Narrative Essay Hooks examples (or simply ways to begin your narrative) that are bound to give you a kick start and force your reader to go beyond the initial sentences!

Some popular Narrative Hooks

  1. Hyperbole

I have a thousand pimples on my face

Why does it work? - An exaggeration is bound to create an element of curiosity amongst the readers, thereby forcing them to go through the complete narrative!

  1. Question

Have you ever been to the USA?

Why does it work? - Another way to stir curiosity is by starting the narrative with a question. If at all it is relatable or open-ended, the reader will be forced to read through the end of the narrative!

  1. Do the Talking

You probably don’t want to know how I escaped from that jungle.

Why does it work? - The best way perhaps to engage a reader is by literally talking to him/her. Fascinatingly talk to the reader. Without a doubt, the reader will gobble up your narrative!

  1. Alliteration

“Billy bounced the ball at the backyard barbeque”.

Why does it work? - Needless to say, the B’s caught your attention, didn’t they? An alliteration quite simply is said to occur when the words in a sentence begin with the same alphabet. This has a unique appeal to the readers and will hook them onto your narrative.

  1. Quotes

“Two roads diverged in the woods, and I-I took the one less traveled by, and that has made all the difference”

Why does it work? - These quotes are famous for a reason. In a few words, they convey numerous feelings, thoughts, and emotions. Using this as a narrative hook will arrest your reader into your mind through the author’s mind.

  1. Figure of Speech

At the drop of a hat, I made that life-changing decision.

Why does it work? - Using a figure of speech such as an idiom will bring out the uniqueness in your writing, thereby leaving the reader asking for more. The way a figure of speech delivers a message in another context is bound to tingle the reader’s curiosity.

  1. Sounds (onomatopoeia)

Ssshhhhhhhhhhhhh! She exclaimed.

Why does it work? - Hearing a sound is normal, but writing a sound is unique and thus it draws attention towards the narrative. Use these sounds in your favor, for you never know when “aarrgh”, “hahaha”, “oops” steal the show away!

  1. Numbers

One in four of the USA’s 22 million people were born overseas!

Why does it work? - A fact with numbers is bound to catch attention. The difference is stark and clearly visible! “10 popular narrative hooks” vs. “Popular Narrative Essay hooks”-which one would you rather read?

  1. Setting the scene

The night was pitch black with little diamonds adorning the sky. The bare trees swayed in the wind, mirroring her emotions.

Why does it work? - You did go beyond the first line? Didn’t you? Pretty self-explanatory!

  1. Bold. Italics. Capitals

“STOP IT ALREADY!”

The conventional, fool-proof old-school attention grabber! Bold the text! It screams out emotions and demands recognition!

Why does it work? - Hopefully, by now you have the basic knowledge and are confident enough to start with your narrative!
